Assignment 3: timelines and distributions


The following button influences the visualization for all the plots in the page.


Temperature trend over the years - line chart

In this plot, we visualize the monthly temperature trends for a selected state over the chosen years.
For each of the selected years we plot 2 lines and 1 scatter plot representing respectivly the maximum, the minimum and the average temperature fluctuations over the months.
The temperatures on the y-axis are in Celsius and it is possible to hover on each of the points to show a tooltip with the exact temperature value of that point and its conversion in Fahrenheit.
With the combination of buttons at the top of the page, it is possible to change the selected state or add/remove different years, with a maximum of 10 at the same time.
To help differentiate the numerous years that could be present on the plot, a legend that associates the color of each line with its respective year is shown.
In addition, to further help with this problematic, it is possible to click on 1 or more of the colors on the legend to isolate its respective years and visualize only that one on the plot.

Temperature trend over the years - radar chart

Here we have 3 different radar charts to showcase the monthly temperature trends for a selected state over the chosen years.
Each of the radar charts is used for the visualization of one among maximum, minimum and average temperatures.

Temperature distribution over the years

Here we have a ridgeline chart where the horizontal axis represents the temperature scale in Celsius.
On that axis two data densities are shown, respectively for minimum (in blue) and maximum (in red) temperatures.
The height and shape of the "ridges" illustrate the concentration and spread of data on the given year.
In the visualization, two green vertical lines mark minimum and maximum values in temperature over all years for that state.
With the combination of buttons at the top of the page, it is possible to change the selected state or add/remove different years, with a maximum of 10 at the same time.
