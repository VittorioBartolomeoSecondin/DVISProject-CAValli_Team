About NEETs

Welcome to our broad overview of the NEET phenomenon through which we would like to highlight its most important aspects.
First of all, you may be wondering what a NEET is.

A NEET, an acronym for "Not in Education, Employment, or Training", is a person who is unemployed and not receiving an education or vocational training. ― Wikipedia

The classification originated in the United Kingdom in the late 1990s, and its use has spread, in varying degrees, to other countries all over the world.

The NEET concept has been widely used as an indicator to inform youth-oriented policies on employability, education, training and also social inclusion in the EU Member States since 2010.

The NEET category includes:
- individuals without a job and seeking one (unemployed)
- individuals without a job and not seeking one (inactive)
It is usually age-bounded to exclude people in old-age retirement.

Poverty (AROPE)

At risk of poverty or social exclusion, abbreviated as AROPE, corresponds to the sum of persons who are either at risk of poverty, or severely materially and socially deprived or living in a household with a very low work intensity. People are included only once even if they are in more than one of the situations mentioned above.
