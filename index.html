About NEETs

Welcome to our broad overview of the NEET phenomenon through which we would like to highlight its most important aspects.
First of all, you may be wondering what a NEET is.

A NEET, an acronym for "Not in Education, Employment, or Training", is a person who is unemployed and not receiving an education or vocational training. ― Wikipedia

The classification originated in the United Kingdom in the late 1990s, and its use has spread, in varying degrees, to other countries, including Japan, South Korea, China, Taiwan, Serbia, Canada, and the United States.

The NEET category includes individuals without a job and seeking one, as well as individuals without a job and not seeking one.
It is usually age-bounded to exclude people in old-age retirement.

In Europe

In this first part of our analysis, the focus will be on the European level.
We are going to present a comparison among 34 countries belonging to the European continent for the time span between 2009 and 2022.

The target will be getting an overview of the phenomenon and identifying countries suffering from it the most.
An additional objective will be understanding whether NEETs are increasing or decreasing over the considered period of time.
